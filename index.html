Animals are fascinating creatures. They have a wide variety of behaviors that make them similar and unique. Here, we will explore those different animal attributes by examining specific animals contained within our expansive and wide ranging habitats.

    Bears

    Bears are large mammals belonging to the family Ursidae and are found in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. They are typically characterized by their stocky bodies, strong limbs, large paws with claws, and shaggy fur. Bears are generally omnivores, though their diets vary widely depending on the species, the season, and available food sources.

    • Ollie

      • Ollie is an angry black bear that exhibits a variety of behaviors that signal its agitation or aggression, which are usually in response to feeling threatened, cornered, or protecting its territory or cubs.

    • Mona

      • Mona is a curious panda bear that likes to play. More social than Ollie, Mona spends her time eating bamboo and harassing zoo keepers.

    Giraffes

    Giraffes are fascinating creatures known for their long necks and unique appearance. They are the tallest land animals, with males (called bulls) standing up to 18 feet (5.5 meters) tall, while females (called cows) are slightly shorter. Giraffes are native to the savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands of Africa, where they primarily feed on leaves, fruits, and flowers from tall trees, especially acacias.

    • Frankie

      • Frankie is a lazy giraffe that spends most of its time sleeping, grazing, and necking with other giraffes.

    • Coconut

      • Coconut is a hyperactive giraffe. He swings his neck and strikes others to establish dominance while continually searching for mates.

    Lions

    Lions (Panthera leo) are large carnivorous mammals that belong to the Felidae family. They are often referred to as the "king of the jungle," although they actually inhabit savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands, primarily in Africa, with a small population in the Gir Forest of India.

    • Mella

      • Mella is a beta female that mostly keeps to herself. She stays in the corners of the habitat and will do anything to avoid conflict.

    • Karl

      • Karl spends his days sleeping, fighting, eating, and marking his territory. He is cantankerous and always finds a way to stay in the middle of situations. Some lions in the African savannah have been known to go rogue and turn into maneaters after humans intruded on and proceeded to systematically destroy their habitat, as was the chilling case of the Ghosts of Tsavo. Luckily, this particular lion is just regularly aggressive and will mainly not stray too far from its regular diet. Best not to tempt fate and agitate it, though ...

    Monkeys

    Monkeys are a diverse group of primates that belong to the infraorder Simiiformes, which also includes apes and humans. They are primarily divided into two main categories: New World monkeys and Old World monkeys.

    • Cookie

      • Cookie is a brightly colored baboon that loves to swing around the trees, fully exploring his habitat, and never to be found in the same place for two minutes. She is hyperactive and always on the move.

    • Earl

      • Earl is a tempermental chimpanzee that constantly butts heads with the other monkeys, often throw his excrement at them and at the zookeepers.

    • Banana Pudding

      • Banana Pudding is an elderly silverback gorilla that sometimes tries to attack the glass dividers between the habitat and the guests. Thankfully, the glass is thick enough to contain the mighty beast, but some habitat glass has been known to begin to crack from the thrown around full weight of an 800 lbs gorilla.
